Phase 1 Validate retrieval on real content 1–2 days
Ingest 10–20 of your actual tutorial videos via YouTube URLs or Drive downloads
Run your 20–30 most common Zendesk ticket types as queries
Does the right video come back? Is the timestamp useful?
Note where retrieval fails — wrong video, wrong segment, no result
Exit criteria
At least 70% of test queries return a relevant video + timestamp. If not, the embedding or chunking strategy needs work before building anything on top.
Phase 2 Google Drive connector 3–5 days
OAuth flow to connect a Drive folder
Watch folder for video files — download, transcribe, ingest via existing pipeline
Handle Loom links in Drive (transcript API vs Whisper fallback)
Re-use existing idempotency — skip already-ingested files on re-scan
Exit criteria
Point it at your actual Drive folder, it ingests everything without manual steps.
Phase 3 Zendesk sidebar app 1–2 weeks
Zendesk Apps Framework (ZAF) sidebar — reads current ticket subject + body
Queries your FastAPI backend, returns top 2–3 video results with timestamps
One-click "insert link" drops a timestamped URL into the reply composer
Deploy backend publicly (Railway or Fly.io) so the sidebar can reach it
This is the only infrastructure work that blocks this phase
Exit criteria
A support agent can open a ticket and see relevant video suggestions without leaving Zendesk. You use it yourself on real tickets.
Phase 4 Productise for other companies 4–6 weeks
Auth + multi-tenancy — per-company namespacing in ChromaDB + SQLite
Onboarding flow — connect Drive, connect Zendesk, ingest, done
Stripe billing — seat-based or usage-based, your call
Zendesk Marketplace listing
Distribution channel you get for free — worth the submission effort
Exit criteria
A company you don't know can sign up, connect their Drive and Zendesk, and be querying their own video library within 10 minutes.
